Self-Assessment Tool (SAT)

This online tool simplifies the initial assessment process.


It offers a clear and efficient way to understand whether an applicant meets the basic criteria before submitting a formal application.
What is the MOM Self-Assessment Tool?

The SAT is an online resource provided by Singapore's MOM. It allows users to input key details about a prospective candidate, such as:

  • Educational qualifications.
  • Proposed salary.
  • Work experience.
  • Nationality.
The tool then analyzes the data against MOM’s guidelines and policies for work pass eligibility, providing a preliminary assessment of whether the individual meets the requirements.
What Does the SAT Do?
    The SAT serves several important functions:
  1. Preliminary Eligibility Check:It provides a yes-or-no indication of whether a candidate is likely to qualify for an EP or S Pass.
  2. Guidance on Requirements:The tool highlights which factors (e.g., salary, qualifications) may need improvement for eligibility, offering actionable insights.
  3. Streamlines the Application Process:By identifying eligibility upfront, employers and applicants can avoid unnecessary rejections, saving time and resources.
How Can the SAT Help?
The SAT benefits both employers and candidates in various ways:
    For Employers:
  • Simplifies Recruitment: By pre-assessing candidates, employers can focus on those who are likely to qualify.
  • Enhances Efficiency: Reduces the administrative burden of processing ineligible applications.
  • Compliance Assurance: Helps employers align with MOM’s policies, reducing the risk of non-compliance.
    • For Applicants:
  • Clarity on Eligibility: Individuals can self-assess to understand if they meet the criteria before applying.
  • Prepares for Negotiations: The tool highlights gaps (e.g., insufficient salary or qualifications) that can be discussed with potential employers.
  • Boosts Confidence: Knowing one’s eligibility increases the likelihood of submitting a strong application.
    Limitations of the SAT While the SAT is a powerful tool, it’s essential to note its limitations:
  1. Indicative Only: The SAT results are not a guarantee of approval, as MOM reviews applications holistically, considering additional factors such as quotas and company compliance history.
  2. Updates in Policy: The tool reflects the latest criteria but may not capture upcoming changes in MOM regulations.
  3. Complex Scenarios: Unique cases, such as applicants with non-standard qualifications or niche job roles, may require manual review by MOM.
    Using the SAT
    To access the SAT, visit the MOM website. Follow these steps:
  1. Input details about the candidate and position.
  2. Review the assessment results.
  3. Adjust parameters if necessary to explore alternate scenarios.
